Week Six

Prompt:Write a Project 2 brief. What are your plans? Why have you choses your particular subject. What are three things you hope to improve/highlight with the new design? What interactions are you considering?

Response:I chose Feral Cat Coalition of Oregon, they are a great organization that helps trap-neuter-release feral cats all on donations and volunteer time. I think the current website is extremely cookie cutter and seems a bit dated. It doesn't reflect the work that the organziation does. I used to volunteer with them and its a very grassroots community feel with a lot of young people volunteering their time. The city has abandoned the feral cat issue and this organization fights to humanely help the problem. Something about it reminds me of the sort of underground work people do in the zine world, so i decided to channel that look and add some color. I also found the links a little hard to find. They should be lumped into the most important reasons people visit: making an appointment, volunteering, donations etc. and then all other resources in another area.

Inspiring site of the week: https://screamintothevoid.com/

Week Eight

Prompt: Thoughts on mobile responsiveness design article: I really found myself taking a step back with my project 2 website after reading this article. It makes a lot of sense to design for the lower broswer first. The concept of "progressive advancement" definitely seems to make more sense to me than "Progressive Advancement". Even though I think our natural tendencies are to design desktop first. I am willing to bet the next generation of designers will intuitively design mobile first because now everybody has smartphones and might not even own a desktop computer.
